## What Counts as Commercial Use?

Commercial Use includes any use:

- **By a business, organisation, or public body** — regardless of size
- **Intended to generate revenue** or support business operations
- **In production environments** — internal or external

- **By employees or contractors** acting on behalf of a commercial entity

A Commercial License is negotiated per customer and may include:

- **Internal business use** — deploy for your team or organisation
- **Rebranding and white-labelling** — customise the UI, name, and branding
- **Modification and extension** — build custom features on top of the platform
- **Organisation-wide deployment** — no per-seat limits (scope defined per agreement)
- **Priority support** — direct access to the author for technical guidance
- **Update entitlements** — access to new versions for a defined period

The specific terms, scope, and pricing are defined in each Commercial License
agreement.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I try it before buying a license?

Yes. The default license permits evaluation and proof-of-concept use for up to
30 days. If you need more time, let us know.

### Can I modify the code?

For personal use: yes, freely. For commercial use: yes, with a Commercial
License. Modifications remain subject to the license terms.

### Can I redistribute my modified version?

Not without a Commercial License that explicitly permits redistribution. The
default license does not allow redistribution.

### Do contributions I make to the project affect my license?

No. Contributing code does not grant you a Commercial License. Contributions
are licensed under the project's inbound=outbound model (see CONTRIBUTING.md).

### What happens if my Commercial License expires?

You may continue using the version you had at expiry, but you will not receive
updates or support. Renewal terms are defined in your agreement.

### Is there a free tier for startups?

Contact us. We're open to discounted or deferred licensing for early-stage
startups on a case-by-case basis.
